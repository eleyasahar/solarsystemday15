The Solar System

Explore the cosmis wonder of our planetary neighborhood.

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun.

  • Distance from Sun: 58 million km
  • Year Length: 88 Earth days
  • Fact: Its surface experience extreme tempratures.

Venus

The second planet from the Sun, often called earth's sister planet.

  • Distance from Sun: 108 million km
  • Year Length: 225 Earth days
  • Fact: Has a runaway greenhouse effect.

Earth

The thrid planet from the sun and the only known to harbor life.

  • Distance from Sun: 150 million km
  • Year Length: 365.25 Earth days
  • Fact: About 71% of its surface is water.

Mars

The "Red Planet," known for its reddish hue and two small moon.

  • Distance from Sun: 288 million km
  • Year Length: 687 Earth days
  • Fact: Home to olypus mons, the largest volcano.

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with a famous Great Red spot storm.

  • Distance from Sun: 778 million km
  • Year Length: 11.86 Earth days
  • Fact: Its Great Red spot is a storm larger than Earth.

Saturn

The sixth planet, best known for its magnificent system of rings

  • Distance from Sun: 1.4 billion km
  • Year Length: 29.4 Earth days
  • Fact: Its rings are made of ice particles and rock.

Uranus

An ice giant that rotates on its side, appearing to role on its orbit .

  • Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km
  • Year Length: 84 Earth days
  • Fact: Has 27 known moons and a faint ring system.

Neptune

The fartest planet from the sun and the windiest in the solar system.

  • Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km
  • Year Length: 165 Earth days
  • Fact: Its great dark spot is a storm the size of Earth.

Blackhole

A region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that that nothing, not even light, can escape.

  • Formed from the collapse of a massive star.
  • Fact: It has a boundary called the event horizon.
  • Size: Can be as small as an atom or millions of times the sun's mass.

Galaxy

A massive, gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter.

  • Our Galaxy: The Milky Way
  • Types: spiral, elliptical, and irregular.
  • Fact: Galaxies can collide and merge.

Important Space Facts

  • The Sun makes up over 99.8% of the total mass of the solar system.
  • A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus.
  • The highest mountain known is on Mars, called Olympus Mons.
  • The universe is estimated to be around 13.8 billion years old.
  • There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all of Earth's beaches.
